Florence Nightingale

Florence Nightingale, c. 1860

Florence led an impressive life - in particular pioneering modern nursing, but also impacting other areas such as applied statistics and data visualisation.

Notable dates and key achievements include:
  • 1820: Florence is born to a wealthy British family in the Italian city of Florence
  • 1853: Became superintendent at the Institute for the Care of Sick Gentlewomen in London
  • 1854: Volunteered to be a nurse in the Crimean War for the British
  • 1855: Used novel visual infographics such as the polar area diagram to graphically represent causes of mortality in the war
  • 1855: Implemented hygiene practices and reform practices leading to a reduced death rate from 42% to 2%
  • 1856: Post-war, established a Nursing Training School still active today as part of the King's College London
  • 1859: First female member elected to the Royal Stastical Society
  • 1859: Wrote the influential Notes on Nursing which served as the key curricula of nursing for nursing schools throughout Britain, and eventually internationally
  • 1874: Became honorary member of the American Statistical Society
  • 1883: Inaugural recipient of the Royal Red Cross, an award for exceptional services in military nursing
  • 1907: First woman awarded the Order of Merit
  • 1910: Died in her sleep, aged 90

Florence Nightingale's Wikipedia page has much more detail if you are interested.
